Group velocity 1

Adding two waves with slightly different wavenumbers.

See section 3.10 in the lecture notes. We argued that a simple way of examining the influence of dispersion was to consider the sum of two periodic signals of "nearly equal" frequency and wavelength (or equivalently wave number). This gave rise to what we defined to be a wave 'group' which had its own 'group velocity'. In the animations below we look at the evolution of the sum of two waves for a few different dispersion relations.

Here is the superposition of two waves where the dispersion relation has been chosen to match that of infinite depth, linearised, free-surface gravity waves. In this case c_g = c / 2 (ie, the green point is moving at twice the speed of the blue one).
